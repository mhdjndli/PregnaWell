If you've ever been told "your hormones are off" without anyone explaining *which*
hormones, or *why*, this post is for you. Almost every modern fertility issue
(irregular cycles, anovulation, low progesterone, PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhea)
ends up tracing back to one feedback loop: the **HPO axis**.

## The three players

- **H: Hypothalamus.** A region of your brain that monitors stress, energy,
  sleep, and body fat, then sends pulses of GnRH to your pituitary.
- **P: Pituitary.** A small gland that responds to GnRH by releasing FSH and LH,
  the two hormones that talk directly to your ovaries.
- **O: Ovaries.** They respond to FSH and LH by maturing follicles, ovulating,
  and producing estrogen and progesterone, which feed back to the brain.

## Why it can break

The HPO axis is exquisitely sensitive to:

- Chronic under-eating or over-exercising
- Persistent psychological stress
- Disturbed sleep
- Insulin resistance and PCOS-pattern signaling
- Thyroid dysfunction

Any of these can quiet the GnRH pulse, and once GnRH goes quiet, ovulation goes
with it.

## What you can do this week

1. **Eat enough.** Especially carbohydrates. The hypothalamus reads low energy as
   "now is not a safe time to reproduce."
2. **Sleep on a schedule.** Even 7 hours, same window each night.
3. **Track your cycle.** Length, ovulation signs, luteal phase. You're looking for
   *patterns*, not perfection.
