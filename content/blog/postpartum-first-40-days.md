Almost every traditional culture has some version of the same idea: a 40-day
postpartum window where the new mother is *kept warm, kept fed, and kept close*.
Modern obstetric research increasingly agrees with that intuition.

## What's actually happening in your body

In the first 6 weeks after birth, you are healing more tissue, more rapidly, than
at almost any other time in adult life. Your uterus contracts back from
pregnancy size. Your blood volume normalizes. Your hormones recalibrate from
pregnancy levels to either lactation or pre-pregnancy levels. Your pelvic floor
begins its slow rebuild.

## Three things that disproportionately matter

1. **Warmth.** Warm food, warm clothing, warm fluids. Cold drains energy your body
   is using to heal.
2. **Iron and protein.** Both are being actively used to rebuild. Soups, stews,
   broth-based meals, and iron-rich greens belong in heavy rotation.
3. **Boundaries on visitors.** The fourth trimester is not a hosting opportunity.
   You're allowed to be unavailable.

## What we get wrong in the West

The "bounce back" narrative compresses an 8-month recovery into a 6-week window
and then asks you to feel guilty about it. Don't. The data on long-term maternal
health are clear: the women who recover slowly recover better.
